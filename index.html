ABOUT

Tech & Society is a working group formed in 2018 by graduate students at ITP to a community of critical inquiry at ITP/IMA and within the tech community in general. Through weekly meetings, reading groups, and guest speakers, we are working to prefigure a technological future that prioritizes ethics and justice and that better serves a wider societal demographic.

In response to the racism and injustice that led to the murder of George Floyd by a Minneapolis police officer, Tech and Society has committed our summer to rapid response programming in solidarity with Black Lives Matter.

We have organized a protest coordination group, a city council calling campaign, a critical thought book club and a series of workshops. Check out our website to learn how to get involved in these activities and subscribe to the newsletter to receive updates.

Start the Conversation Edit-a-Thon

August 23, 2020
Offered by the Coding Lab

Join with this link: https://nyu.zoom.us/j/6870749012

Start The Conversation is an app to help you have difficult conversations. It was born out of the need we felt to persuade loved ones through conversation to recognize racial injustice in the United States. You can text the app at +1(205)890-3139 with a question or topic and get a relevant response to add to your conversation. You can say things like "Tell me more about police brutality," or "What do you know about infant mortality and race in the US?" or “I want to know about incarceration and race.” The hope is that this app will quickly give you the resources you may need to meaningfully engage in these conversations. We will hold an Edit-A-Thon-style workshop on Sunday July 12th at 2pm where we will edit the database of responses and then hold a second workshop TBD to see how effective the app has been for us and to review the technology behind it.
