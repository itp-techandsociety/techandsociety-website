ABOUT

Tech & Society is a working group formed in 2018 by graduate students at ITP to a community of critical inquiry at ITP/IMA and within the tech community in general. Through weekly meetings, reading groups, and guest speakers, we are working to prefigure a technological future that prioritizes ethics and justice and that better serves a wider societal demographic.

In response to the racism and injustice that led to the murder of George Floyd by a Minneapolis police officer, Tech and Society has committed our summer to rapid response programming in solidarity with Black Lives Matter.

We have organized a protest coordination group, a city council calling campaign, a critical thought book club and a series of workshops. Check out our website to learn how to get involved in these activities and subscribe to the newsletter to receive updates.

BOOK CLUB

The Tech & Society Book Club is currently reading Algorithms of Oppression by Safiya Umoja Noble - please join us!


Date and Time

The book club meets on Tuesdays at 8pm Eastern Time on Zoom starting June 30 through August 4. Please fill out the registration form in order to sign up and receive the zoom link the day of the event: Register Here


The Book

Algorithms of Oppression is available for free through the NYU library system! Under the View Online/EBook Central option you can choose to download the book (pdf/epub), read it online or download chapter pdfs. Please let us know if you have problems accessing it. If you are planning to buy the book, we encourage you to support your independent, local, Black-owned businesses (and maybe buy a few more books too while you're at it!?!)! Want a list of some places to start? Let us know!


What to Read

Here is a breakdown of the readings by week:

  • June 30: Intro + Chapter 1
  • July 7: Chapter 2
  • July 14: Chapter 3 + Chapter 4
